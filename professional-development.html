Reflective Journal

Teaching in a post-pandemic classroom has been both a challenge and a profound learning experience. As a pre-service teacher, I entered the classroom aware of the unique needs and anxieties that students carried with them after years of remote learning and social isolation. I quickly realized that my role extended beyond delivering content; I needed to foster a sense of community, safety, and resilience.

I embraced flexible teaching strategies, blending technology with hands-on activities to accommodate diverse learning preferences. I encourage open dialogue, allowing students to share their experiences and feelings, which helps rebuild trust and engagement. I noticed that students thrived when given opportunities to collaborate, problem-solve, and support one another, reinforcing my belief in constructivist approaches.

Adapting to new health protocols and shifting expectations taught me the importance of patience, empathy, and adaptability. I learned to celebrate small victories—whether it was a student’s breakthrough in understanding or a group’s successful project. This experience has shaped my teaching philosophy, reminding me that education is not just about academic achievement but also about nurturing well-being and community. I am grateful for the resilience I witnessed and the growth I experienced, both as an educator and as a lifelong learner.
