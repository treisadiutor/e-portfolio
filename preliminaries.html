Camarines Norte State College History

The CAMARINES NORTE STATE COLLEGE has originated from the Camarines Norte High School (CNHS) which was established in 1920. On June 23, 1973 Parliamentary Bill No. 2378 was approved and signed into law converting Camarines Norte High School to Camarines Norte National High School (CNNHS). It was on the 23rd day of July 1991 when former Congressman Renato M. Unico introduced House Bill No. 31647 in the House of Representatives during its fifth Regular Session. After it was finally passed by the Senate and the House of Representatives, the Bill was signed into Law by Former President Corazon C. Aquino on April 2, 1992 as Republic Act No. 7352 also known as "An Act Converting the present Camarines Norte National High School in the Municipality of Daet into a State College to be known as the Camarines Norte State College (CNSC) integrating for the purpose the Abaño Pilot Elementary School in Daet, Mercedes School of Fisheries in Mercedes, National Agricultural School in Labo, and Camarines Norte School of Arts and Trades in Jose Panganiban all in the province of Camarines Norte and appropriating funds thereof."

Camarines Norte State College, College of Education History

The Abaño Pilot Elementary School, now Abaño Campus, has an area of 0.8684 hectares. It was established in 1953 and was designated the pilot school of the Division of Camarines Norte in 1959 and later became a regional pilot school by virtue of its good performance in the field of elementary education. Philanthropist Dr. Jose Abaño donated the lot. It houses the Secondary and Elementary Laboratory Schools of the College of Education and the CNSC Training Center.

Student Teacher Responsibilities

  1. Enter with internship contract/agreement.
  2. Notify the Cooperating Teacher in case of absence and have a parent/guardian co-sign the absence/contract.
  3. Report for internship in the cooperating school starting January 20, 2025; complete the required internship period (until April 16, 2025); and comply with completion week requirements (April 30, 2025).
  4. Participate actively in school-related activities while giving priority to academic-related activities.
  5. Observe a proper mentee-mentor relationship with the cooperating teacher, school head, and other teachers by showing due respect at all times.
  6. Be open to suggestions, recommendations, and constructive criticism from the Cooperating Teacher.
  7. Respect the dignity and rights of the pupils/students.
  8. Come prepared at all times.
  9. Project a positive image as a teacher by adhering to the proper dress code, Code of Ethics, and punctuality during all internship activities.
  10. Submit a daily journal of experiences to the Supervising Teacher/Adviser, detailing training activities, encountered problems, and reflections.
  11. Submit Teaching Internship requirements to the Teacher/Adviser (April 2025) and accomplish the Daily Time Record (DTR).
  12. Perform a final demo teaching session with the cooperating teacher and school head serving as raters and observers.
  13. Sign in and out of the logbook managed by the school's PST focal person.

Moreno Integrated School History

Moreno Integrated School is a public secondary school established in the year 1995 and started operating under the authority of the Department of Education the same year. The mandate of Moreno Integrated School is defined by Republic Act 9155, Governance of Basic Education and EFA, which authorizes the Department of Education to establish schools in every barangay as part of the national goal. The school perseveres in upholding the DepEd Mission of protecting and promoting the right of every Filipino to quality, equitable, culture-based, and complete basic education where students learn in a child-friendly, gender-sensitive, safe, and motivating environment; teachers facilitate learning and nurture every learner; administrators and staff ensure an enabling and supportive environment for effective learning; and family, community, and other stakeholders are actively engaged and share the responsibility for developing life-long learners. The Agency's registered office is located at Gov. Panotes Avenue, Barangay VIII, Daet, Camarines Norte.

Introduction

"Being a teacher means being a mother to every child."

As an aspiring educator, I am committed to fostering a classroom environment where students are active participants in their own learning journeys. My teaching philosophy is grounded in the principles of constructivism, which emphasizes that learners construct knowledge through meaningful experiences, collaboration, and reflection.

I believe that students learn best when they are encouraged to explore, question, and connect new ideas to their prior knowledge. In my classroom, I strive to create opportunities for hands-on activities, open-ended discussions, and real-world problem-solving. By acting as a facilitator and guide, I support my students as they take ownership of their learning, develop critical thinking skills, and grow into lifelong learners.

This portfolio showcases my experiences, lesson plans, and reflections as a pre-service teacher. It is a testament to my dedication to creating a dynamic, inclusive, and student-centered learning environment where every learner is empowered to reach their full potential.
